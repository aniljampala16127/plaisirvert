Soil Aeration

Give your lawn room to breathe with our professional core aeration services

What is Soil Aeration?

Soil aeration is the process of creating small holes in your lawn to allow air, water, and nutrients to penetrate the grass roots. This helps the roots grow deeply and produce a stronger, more vigorous lawn.

Why Your Lawn Needs Aeration

Over time, soil becomes compacted due to foot traffic, mowing, and natural settling. This compaction restricts the flow of essential elements that grass needs to grow and thrive. Signs that your lawn may need aeration include:

  • Water puddles after rain
  • Thinning grass or bare patches
  • Heavy thatch (over 1/2 inch)
  • Hard, compacted soil that's difficult to penetrate
  • Heavy clay soil content
  • High traffic areas that show wear

Our Eco-Friendly Approach

At Plaisir Vert, we use core aeration techniques that are gentle on your lawn and the environment. Our specialized equipment removes small plugs of soil from your lawn, creating channels for air, water, and nutrients to reach the root zone more effectively.

Benefits of Professional Soil Aeration

Improved Water Absorption

Reduces water runoff and puddling by allowing water to penetrate deeper into the soil.

Enhanced Root Growth

Creates space for roots to expand, resulting in stronger, more drought-resistant grass.

Reduced Thatch

Helps break down thatch by introducing more oxygen to soil microorganisms.

Natural Pest Resistance

Healthier grass is naturally more resistant to disease and pest problems.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to aerate my lawn?

The best time to aerate cool-season grasses (common in Montreal) is in the early fall or spring when the grass is in its peak growing period. This allows the lawn to heal and fill in any open areas after aeration.

How often should I aerate my lawn?

Most lawns benefit from annual aeration. However, heavily used lawns or those with dense clay soil may need aeration twice a year. During your initial consultation, we can recommend the ideal frequency for your specific lawn.

What should I do after my lawn is aerated?

After aeration, it's an ideal time to overseed and fertilize your lawn since the holes provide direct contact with the soil. Keep the lawn moist but not saturated for the next couple of weeks. The soil plugs can be left on the lawn – they'll break down and return nutrients to the soil.

Will aeration eliminate all my lawn problems?

While aeration is a vital part of lawn maintenance, it's just one component of a comprehensive lawn care program. For best results, aeration should be combined with proper mowing, watering, fertilization, and other practices. Our team can help you develop a complete lawn care plan tailored to your specific needs.
